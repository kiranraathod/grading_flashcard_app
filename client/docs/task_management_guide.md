## Introduction

This guide provides instructions for language models on how to effectively manage tasks and subtasks. Proper task management ensures that all user requests are addressed methodically, comprehensively, and in the right order. Following a structured approach helps in delivering clear, organized, and complete responses to users.

Key benefits of effective task management include:
- Ensuring all parts of a request are addressed
- Maintaining organized workflows
- Providing clarity on progress and completion
- Delivering more comprehensive responses
- Avoiding overlooking important details

## Task Management Workflow

The task management process follows three main phases:

1. **Planning Phase**: Analyze the user's request and break it down into clear, actionable tasks
2. **Execution Phase**: Work through tasks methodically in a logical sequence
3. **Completion Phase**: Verify all tasks are complete and summarize what was accomplished

This structured approach ensures that all aspects of the user's request are properly addressed.

## Task Planning

When a user presents a request, follow these steps to create an effective task plan:

### 1. Analyze the Request

First, thoroughly analyze the user's request to understand:
- The main objective
- Specific requirements or constraints
- Implied tasks that may not be explicitly stated
- The scope and complexity of what's being asked

### 2. Identify Tasks and Subtasks

Break down the request into discrete, manageable tasks:

- **Main Tasks**: Primary components that address major parts of the request
- **Subtasks**: Smaller actions required to complete each main task
- **Dependencies**: Identify which tasks must be completed before others

### 3. Create a Task List

Formulate a clear list of tasks, organized in a logical sequence. For complex requests, consider creating:

- A numbered task list
- Hierarchical organization with main tasks and subtasks
- Clear task descriptions that define what completion looks like

### 4. Example Task Planning

**User Request**: "Can you help me write a blog post about climate change, focusing on recent research and including some data visualizations?"

**Task Plan**:
1. Research recent climate change studies and findings
   - Identify key research from the past 1-2 years
   - Note important statistics and data points
2. Create an outline for the blog post
   - Develop a compelling introduction
   - Organize main sections based on research themes
   - Plan a conclusion with call-to-action
3. Draft content for each section of the blog post
4. Suggest data visualization concepts
   - Identify key data points that would benefit from visualization
   - Describe visualization types appropriate for the data
5. Review and refine the complete blog post

## Task Execution

Once you've created a task plan, execute the tasks methodically:

### 1. Follow Sequential Processing

- Work through tasks in a logical order
- Complete prerequisite tasks before dependent ones
- Inform the user of your current focus to maintain clarity

### 2. Provide Progressive Updates

- For complex or multi-step tasks, provide updates on progress
- When shifting between major tasks, indicate the transition
- If relevant, explain why you're approaching tasks in a particular order

### 3. Maintain Focus and Depth

- Give each task appropriate attention and depth
- Avoid rushing through tasks to reach completion
- Ensure quality in execution at each step

### 4. Example Task Execution

**Task Being Executed**: "Create an outline for the blog post"

"I'll now create an outline for the climate change blog post based on recent research:

**Blog Post Outline: Climate Change - Latest Research and Insights**

I. Introduction
   - Hook: Compelling statistic or recent climate event
   - Brief overview of climate change's current status
   - Thesis statement on the importance of recent research

II. Recent Breakthrough Findings (Main Section 1)
   - Key finding #1 from [specific recent research]
   - Key finding #2 from [specific recent research]
   - Implications of these findings

III. Data Trends and Analysis (Main Section 2)
   - Temperature change data from past decade
   - Sea level rise measurements
   - Extreme weather event frequency changes

IV. Innovative Solutions Emerging from Research (Main Section 3)
   - Technological innovations
   - Policy approaches backed by research
   - Collaborative international research initiatives

V. Conclusion
   - Summary of key points
   - Call to action based on research implications
   - Forward-looking statement on research directions

Next, I'll move on to drafting content for each section of the blog post..."

## Task Completion

As you complete each task, follow these steps to ensure thoroughness:

### 1. Verify Completion

Before marking a task as complete:
- Ensure all aspects of the task have been addressed
- Check that the output meets the requirements specified in the task
- Validate that the completed task aligns with the user's original request

### 2. Summarize Results

When completing significant tasks:
- Briefly summarize what was accomplished
- Highlight key outcomes or deliverables
- Connect the completed task to the overall objective

### 3. Transition to Next Task

After completing a task:
- Clearly indicate the next task to be addressed
- Explain how the next task builds on or relates to the completed task
- If appropriate, check with the user before proceeding to major new tasks

### 4. Example Task Completion

"I've completed the blog post outline with an introduction, three main content sections focusing on recent research, data trends, and emerging solutions, and a conclusion with a call to action. The outline provides a structured approach to presenting climate change research in an engaging format.

Next, I'll begin drafting the actual content for each section of the blog post, starting with the introduction..."

## Handling Subtasks

Complex tasks often require breaking down into subtasks for effective management:

### 1. Identify Subtasks

- Break larger tasks into manageable components
- Ensure each subtask is specific and actionable
- Arrange subtasks in a logical sequence

### 2. Track Subtask Progress

- Address subtasks systematically
- Indicate when transitioning between subtasks
- For complex workflows, provide periodic status updates

### 3. Balance Depth and Breadth

- Give each subtask appropriate attention
- Avoid getting lost in minutiae of a single subtask
- Maintain perspective on how subtasks contribute to the main task

### 4. Example Subtask Handling

**Main Task**: "Research recent climate change studies and findings"

**Subtasks**:
1. "First, I'll identify key research publications from the past 1-2 years regarding climate change."
2. "Now, I'll extract the main findings from each of these studies."
3. "Next, I'll note important statistics and data points that could be featured in the blog post."
4. "Finally, I'll synthesize the research to identify common themes and significant developments."

## Task Prioritization

When managing multiple tasks, prioritization becomes essential:

### 1. Determine Priority Criteria

Consider these factors when prioritizing tasks:
- **Dependencies**: Tasks that others depend on should come first
- **Importance**: Tasks central to the main objective
- **Complexity**: Sometimes complex tasks should be addressed earlier
- **User guidance**: Explicit prioritization from the user

### 2. Explain Prioritization Decisions

When prioritizing:
- Briefly explain your prioritization approach if it's not obvious
- Seek confirmation for your approach on complex requests
- Be flexible if the user suggests a different priority order

### 3. Example Prioritization

"Based on your request, I'll prioritize tasks in this order:

1. First, researching recent climate change studies, as this will provide the foundation for all other content
2. Next, creating the blog post outline to establish the structure
3. Then, drafting the content based on the research and outline
4. Finally, suggesting data visualization concepts to enhance the written content

This approach ensures that each step builds logically on the previous one, creating a cohesive final product."

## Best Practices

To excel at task management, follow these best practices:

### 1. Clarity and Specificity

- Define tasks with clear, specific descriptions
- Avoid vague or overly broad task definitions
- Set clear completion criteria for each task

### 2. Appropriate Granularity

- Break down complex tasks into manageable pieces
- Avoid excessive fragmentation of simple tasks
- Match the level of task breakdown to the complexity of the request

### 3. Communication and Transparency

- Keep the user informed about your task management approach
- Provide appropriate progress updates
- Explain transitions between major tasks

### 4. Adaptability

- Be prepared to adjust tasks based on user feedback
- Reprioritize as new information becomes available
- Add, modify, or remove tasks as needed during execution

### 5. Thoroughness

- Ensure each task is fully completed before moving on
- Verify that all aspects of the original request are addressed
- Review the final output against the initial task list

## Example Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Writing Assistant

**User Request**: "I need to write a cover letter for a marketing position. I have 5 years of experience in content creation and social media management."

**Task Management Approach**:

1. **Task Planning**:
   - Task 1: Gather key information about the marketing position and user's experience
   - Task 2: Create a structure for the cover letter
   - Task 3: Draft the introduction paragraph
   - Task 4: Develop body paragraphs highlighting relevant experience
   - Task 5: Craft a strong conclusion
   - Task 6: Review and refine the complete letter

2. **Task Execution** (example for one task):
   "Now I'll draft the introduction paragraph for your cover letter. A strong introduction should grab attention, mention the specific position you're applying for, and briefly state why you're a good fit..."

3. **Task Completion**:
   "I've completed the cover letter draft with a compelling introduction, two body paragraphs highlighting your content creation and social media management experience, and a confident conclusion. Would you like me to review any specific part of the letter in more detail?"

### Scenario 2: Research Assistant

**User Request**: "Explain the impact of artificial intelligence on healthcare, particularly in diagnostics and treatment planning."

**Task Management Approach**:

1. **Task Planning**:
   - Task 1: Research AI applications in healthcare diagnostics
   - Task 2: Research AI applications in treatment planning
   - Task 3: Identify benefits and improvements from AI integration
   - Task 4: Analyze challenges and limitations
   - Task 5: Synthesize information into a comprehensive explanation
   - Task 6: Provide examples of current implementations

2. **Task Execution** (example):
   "I'll begin by exploring how AI is currently being used in healthcare diagnostics. AI systems are increasingly being implemented in radiology for image analysis..."

3. **Task Completion**:
   "I've provided a comprehensive overview of AI's impact on healthcare diagnostics and treatment planning, covering the major benefits, challenges, and current implementations. Is there any specific aspect you'd like me to elaborate on further?"

By following this task management guide, language models can effectively organize, execute, and complete complex user requests in a systematic and thorough manner.
